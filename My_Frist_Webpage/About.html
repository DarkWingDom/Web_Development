Purpose

This site was created for a study project of the Keyin College "Software Development" course. In this QAP (Quantitative Asseeemnet Practice) we were asked to pick a species of animal, insect, etc. I decided to pick the Northern Flicker.

The reason I chose this species begins with a story between me and my wife. I first seen this Pidgeon sized bird in my back yard in the spring of 2018 (check date). Not knowing it but we had these visitors prior. My wife spoke to me about a noise waking her up on a number of occasions. When I asked her what it sounded like she described it as someone drilling into the house. I thought right away that one of the neighbors had a power tool operating. This continued for some time and my wife was certain that it was directly impacting our house. I looked outside and seen nothing on a couple of occasions. Then one morning I was awoken by the very sound that was described to me. I opened the blinds of my bedroom and there they were, a whole family of Northern Flickers. I didn't know what they were until I researched them.

This is a very interesting field and look forward to continue my learning.
